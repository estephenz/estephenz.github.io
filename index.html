Leading change

Why simply manage change when you can lead change?

As manager's it is our expectation to champion change.

The tools we have are effective in helping us understanding what is happening during the acceptance of a change.

There is a need for a tool to help lead change.

We are in a constently changing environment.

Maintaining a positive working environment through change is challenging.

We need a new perspective. A well planned, consistent approach to moving an idea into a lasting action.

John Kotter created the 8 steps of leading change.

  1. Establish a sense of urgency.

    2. Share the why. Sometimes itâ€™s more than words. For example with FSD the urgency is felt now that we do not see as many enhancements coming through and we have reminders that we have outgrown SHARE. The urgency (why) is tangible. Change is not comfortable sometimes an organization needs to be intentional when demonstrating that change is needed. This means removing workarounds taking away extras.

  2. Build and Maintain a guiding Coalition

    3. Who is the sponsor? Strategic initiatives group; Core function Sr manager. Who has the responsibility and influence to back and support the change effort.

  3. Formulate a strategic vision, create a plan of action and a brief description of the desired result.

    4. AFS Vision- easy; engaged; economical. A brief explanation of where you want to be when the change is complete. This should be clear, inspiring and BRIEF! 5 minutes or less. Capture the audience.

  4. Communicate broadly from various channels.
  5. Empower broad based action
  6. Generate Short Term Wins
  7. Never Let up, Don't declare victory too soon.
  8. Anchor change to the culture.

Imagine feeling prepared and supported to lead change by inspiring others with a clear vision of the end result.
